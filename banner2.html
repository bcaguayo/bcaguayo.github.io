Know Your Flags: A Queer Banner Compendium

Pride Flag

Introduced in 1978, the Pride Flag is the most recognizable symbol of the LGBTQ+ community. Its six colors represent:

  • Red: Life
  • Orange: Healing
  • Yellow: Sunlight
  • Green: Nature
  • Blue: Harmony
  • Violet: Spirit

Progress Flag

Introduced in 2018, the Progress Flag builds upon the traditional Pride Flag by incorporating a chevron of black and brown stripes to represent marginalized LGBTQ+ communities of color, as well as the colors of the Transgender Pride Flag to represent the transgender community.

Bisexual Flag

Introduced in 1998, the Bisexual Flag represents the bisexual community. Its three colors represent:

  • Pink: Attraction to the same sex
  • Purple: Attraction to both sexes
  • Blue: Attraction to the opposite sex

Gay Men's Flag

Introduced in 1995, the Gay Men's Flag represents the gay male community. Its seven colors represent:

  • Red: Life
  • Orange: Healing
  • Yellow: Sunlight
  • Green: Nature
  • Turquoise: Magic/Art
  • Indigo: Serenity
  • Violet: Spirit

Lesbian Flag

Introduced in 1999, the Lesbian Flag represents the lesbian community. Its five colors represent:

  • Dark orange: Non-conformity
  • Orange: Independence
  • Light orange: Community
  • White: Unique relationships to womanhood
  • Dark pink: Serenity and peace

Pansexual Flag

Introduced in 2010, the Pansexual Flag represents the pansexual community
